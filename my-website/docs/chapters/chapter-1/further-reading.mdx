# Further Reading

## Academic Literature and Research Papers

### Foundational Papers in Physical AI
- **Brooks, R. A. (1991).** "Intelligence without representation." *Artificial Intelligence*, 47(1-3), 139-159. This seminal paper introduced the concept of embodied cognition and reactive robotics, foundational to Physical AI.

- **Pfeifer, R., & Scheier, C. (1999).** "Understanding Intelligence." *MIT Press*. This book explores how intelligence emerges from the interaction between an agent and its environment, providing theoretical foundations for Physical AI.

- **Siciliano, B., & Khatib, O. (Eds.). (2016).** "Springer Handbook of Robotics." *Springer*. This comprehensive handbook covers all aspects of robotics relevant to Physical AI, including perception, control, and human-robot interaction.

### Recent Advances in Physical AI
- **Saxena, A., Driemeyer, J., & Ng, A. Y. (2008).** "Learning 3D object orientation from images." *Proceedings of the IEEE Conference on Computer Vision and Pattern Recognition (CVPR)*. This paper demonstrates how robots can learn to perceive 3D object properties from 2D images.

- **Levine, S., Pastor, P., Krizhevsky, A., & Quillen, D. (2016).** "Learning hand-eye coordination for robotic grasping with deep learning and large-scale data collection." *The International Journal of Robotics Research*, 35(4), 423-436. This work shows how deep learning can be applied to Physical AI tasks.

- **Toussaint, M. (2015).** "Logic-based specification languages for intelligent manipulation robots." *KI - KÃ¼nstliche Intelligenz*, 29(4), 333-340. This paper discusses how to specify and plan complex manipulation tasks in Physical AI systems.

## Books and Textbooks

### Core Physical AI and Robotics
- **Thrun, S., Burgard, W., & Fox, D. (2005).** "Probabilistic Robotics." *MIT Press*. Essential reading for understanding uncertainty in Physical AI systems, covering localization, mapping, and planning.

- **Spong, M. W., Hutchinson, S., & Vidyasagar, M. (2020).** "Robot Modeling and Control." *John Wiley & Sons*. Comprehensive coverage of robot kinematics, dynamics, and control theory.

- **Russell, S., & Norvig, P. (2020).** "Artificial Intelligence: A Modern Approach" (4th ed.). *Pearson*. While not exclusively about Physical AI, this textbook provides essential background in AI that is applied in Physical AI systems.

### Specialized Topics
- **Murray, R. M., Li, Z., & Sastry, S. S. (1994).** "A Mathematical Introduction to Robotic Manipulation." *CRC Press*. Mathematical foundations for robot manipulation, essential for understanding technical foundations of Physical AI.

- **Choset, H., et al. (2005).** "Principles of Robot Motion: Theory, Algorithms, and Implementations." *MIT Press*. Covers motion planning and navigation, core components of Physical AI.

- **Hogan, N., & Sternad, D. (2012).** "On the stability of manipulative action." *Journal of Motor Behavior*, 44(5), 323-335. Discusses the physics of manipulation, relevant for Physical AI systems.
